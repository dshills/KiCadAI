Create a two-layer protected USB-C powered BMP280 I2C breakout on a 100 mm by 75 mm board with project acceptance set to erc-drc. Use a power-only USB-C receptacle with two mandatory CC pull-down components: connect CC1 to ground through its own 5.1 kOhm resistor and connect CC2 to ground through a separate 5.1 kOhm resistor. Route raw VBUS through a 500 mA fuse to a protected 5 V rail. Connect a 5 V TVS diode and a 10 uF bulk capacitor from the protected 5 V rail to ground, not on the raw VBUS side.

Use the verified AP2112K 3.3 V regulator. Tie its enable input to the protected 5 V rail, add a 1 uF input capacitor and 2.2 uF output capacitor, and generate a 3.3 V rail. Use the verified Bosch BMP280 LGA-8 component in I2C mode: connect VDD, VDDIO, and CSB to 3.3 V; connect both GND pins to ground; and connect SDO through a 10 kOhm resistor to ground for address 0x76. Add a local 100 nF sensor decoupling capacitor. Add separate 4.7 kOhm SDA and SCL pull-ups to 3.3 V.

Use a four-pin output header ordered as 3.3 V, SDA, SCL, and ground. Use 0.8 mm traces for raw VBUS, protected 5 V, and ground. Use 0.5 mm traces for 3.3 V. Use 0.25 mm signal traces for CC1, CC2, SDA, SCL, and the SDO address strap. Explicitly mark the protected 5 V and ground nets as externally driven schematic power sources. Lay the schematic out left to right with USB-C input and protection on the left, the regulator next, the BMP280 and local support parts after it, and the header on the right; keep positive rails above and ground below. Use at least 25.4 mm of schematic group spacing so adjacent multi-pin symbols cannot share pin anchors.

Mark every named net in this design as required so routing and connectivity validation fail closed if any endpoint is omitted.

Define bounded PCB regions for power input/protection, sensor circuitry, and the output connector, and reference only those defined regions in placement hints. Express PCB placement using relative constraints rather than absolute coordinates: put the USB-C receptacle at the left board edge; keep the fuse within 15 mm of the receptacle; keep each mandatory CC pull-down within 10 mm of the receptacle; keep the protected-side TVS diode within 5 mm of the fuse output and the bulk capacitor within 10 mm of the fuse; keep the regulator within 15 mm of the fuse; keep its input and output capacitors within 5 mm of the regulator; keep the BMP280 within 20 mm of the regulator; keep its decoupling capacitor within 5 mm of the sensor; keep the I2C pull-ups and SDO strap within 8 mm of the sensor; and put the output header at the right board edge. Assign higher priority to the USB-C receptacle, fuse, protection parts, regulator, regulator capacitors, sensor, sensor decoupling capacitor, and output header than to pull-ups and straps. Leave routing channels between the power-input, sensor, and output regions.

For schematic placement, use group ranks to establish the left-to-right USB-C, regulation, sensor, and output stage order. Keep component-relative constraints sparse: anchor the regulator stage after protection, the sensor stage after regulation, and the output after the sensor. Place the SDA pull-up above the sensor; place the SCL pull-up above the sensor and to the right of the SDA pull-up so their pin anchors cannot overlap. Orient both pull-up resistors vertically (`rotated_90`) with their power-side pins above and their SDA/SCL signal-side pins below. Do not form any longer horizontal `right_of` chain through the sensor, address strap, or decoupling capacitor, and do not add unnecessary `near` relations for those support parts; let the group, power, signal, and ground lanes provide their spacing.

If a schematic bus is used, define only one I2C bus containing SDA and SCL. Do not put 3.3 V, 5 V, VBUS, or ground nets in a bus; those are scalar power nets and must remain in the power and ground lanes.
