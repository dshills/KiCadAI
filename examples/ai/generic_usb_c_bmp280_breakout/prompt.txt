Create a two-layer protected USB-C powered BMP280 I2C breakout on a 100 mm by 75 mm board with project acceptance set to erc-drc. Use a power-only USB-C receptacle with separate 5.1 kOhm CC1 and CC2 pull-down resistors to ground. Route raw VBUS through a 500 mA fuse to a protected 5 V rail. Connect a 5 V TVS diode and a 10 uF bulk capacitor from the protected 5 V rail to ground, not on the raw VBUS side.

Use the verified AP2112K 3.3 V regulator. Tie its enable input to the protected 5 V rail, add a 1 uF input capacitor and 2.2 uF output capacitor, and generate a 3.3 V rail. Use the verified Bosch BMP280 LGA-8 component in I2C mode: connect VDD, VDDIO, and CSB to 3.3 V; connect both GND pins to ground; and connect SDO through a 10 kOhm resistor to ground for address 0x76. Add a local 100 nF sensor decoupling capacitor. Add separate 4.7 kOhm SDA and SCL pull-ups to 3.3 V.

Use a four-pin output header ordered as 3.3 V, SDA, SCL, and ground. Use 0.8 mm traces for raw VBUS, protected 5 V, and ground; use 0.5 mm for 3.3 V; and use signal-width traces for CC1, CC2, SDA, SCL, and the SDO address strap. Explicitly mark the protected 5 V and ground nets as externally driven schematic power sources. Lay the schematic out left to right with USB-C input and protection on the left, the regulator next, the BMP280 and local support parts after it, and the header on the right; keep positive rails above and ground below. Use at least 25.4 mm of schematic group spacing so adjacent multi-pin symbols cannot share pin anchors. Define bounded PCB regions for power input/protection, sensor circuitry, and the output connector, and reference only those defined regions in placement hints.
